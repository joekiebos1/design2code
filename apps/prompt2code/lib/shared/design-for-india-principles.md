# Design for India Principles

## Core principles

1. Made in India for India — Built from an Indian starting point. Desirability standard is Apple: equally desirable but warm, communal, present. Never "good for India" — the best choice, full stop.

2. Connection is our DNA — Family and community are the point of life. Ground use cases in family and friends. Community is felt, not named.

3. Aspiration and value together — Lead with experience. Let value be proof, never the hook. Access conditions never open the story.

4. Digital life as celebration — Festivals, cricket, Bollywood, family milestones. The celebration feeling lives in tone, use cases, and imagery simultaneously.

5. Trust is earned through relationship — Stories must feel like a trusted recommendation, not a broadcast. Specific, honest, personal.

6. Emotion is processed before logic — The emotional opening is the condition under which all rational proof is received. Never open with features.

7. Specificity signals honesty — "Works in Bhojpuri" is believed. "Supports all Indian languages" is not. Name the specific thing.

8. Aspiration is collective — Show the product being shared, not just used alone.

9. The new and the familiar must coexist — Warmth alongside ambition. Never force a choice between tradition and modernity.

10. Value is understood relationally — Lead with experience. Let value land as revelation, not hook.

## Family as lens — use with judgment, not by default

Family is the natural context for many Jio products but not all. Before applying the family lens to a use case, ask: is family genuinely central to how this product is used, or is this primarily an individual product?

Products where family is the primary lens: shared entertainment, home connectivity, communication, gifting. Use the family lens freely throughout the story.

Products where family is secondary: personal devices, vehicle technology, professional tools, individual services. The individual is the primary user. Family use cases may still appear where they genuinely add a dimension — where a specific feature or moment is meaningfully enriched by the family context — but should not be the default framing for every block.

The test: does the family reference make this specific use case more resonant and true — or is it being applied because family always feels warm and Indian? If it's the latter, let the individual product truth stand on its own.

## India is the lens, not the subject

Indian context should make the story feel true and specific — not exotic or performative. A story that reaches for Indian metaphors when a direct product truth would land better has overcorrected.

The test: does this Indian reference make the product more believable and more felt — or does it make the story feel like it is trying to sound Indian?

Use Indian references when:
- A specific cultural moment genuinely illuminates the product's value
- The Indian reality (a language, a network, a family structure) is the actual RTB
- The moment of recognition is stronger with it than without it

Do not use Indian references when:
- The product claim is already specific and credible on its own
- The reference is decorative rather than load-bearing
- It distances the visitor from the product rather than bringing them closer to it

Not every block needs to be poetic or culturally grounded. Rational proof blocks should be clear and direct. Reserve Indian cultural references for the emotional peaks — the hook, the proud-of moment, and the close. Let the middle blocks breathe and be plain where plain is right.

## Connectivity is a promise, not a problem

Jio built the network that connects India. Jio products never frame connectivity as a challenge to work around — they express it as a promise kept.

Never reference slow data, poor signal, or unreliable connectivity as problems. Instead: Jio keeps you connected everywhere, always. The product works across every kind of Jio network because Jio built those networks to reach everyone.

When a product's connectivity reach is an RTB, frame it as a statement of ambition: "Jio ensures you are always connected" — not as a concession to infrastructure limitations.

## India-specific RTB guidance

WHAT WE'RE PROUD OF: the India/Jio-specific decision that only exists because this was built here, for here.
Format: name the decision + name the Indian reality it serves.
Example: "Built to keep you connected everywhere — adaptive streaming ensures the experience never stops, across every kind of Jio network."
Surfaces at the emotional peak of Engage. Lands as intimacy, not specification.
Wrong: "Built for India." — must name what was actually built and why it matters for Indian users.

SOCIAL RTB: editorial credibility, cultural specificity at Indian moments.
Example: "In-house Indian editorial team builds Diwali playlists two weeks before — not algorithmic, not outsourced."
Wrong: "Loved by millions."
